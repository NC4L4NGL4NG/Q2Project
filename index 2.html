Kare-Kare

Ingredients

1 kg oxtail (or beef, tripe, or pork) 1 banana heart, sliced 1 bundle of string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces 1 small eggplant, sliced 1/4 cup peanut butter 1/4 cup ground toasted rice 1 tbsp annatto powder (or oil) 4 cups water (or beef broth) 2 tbsp fish sauce Salt and pepper to taste Shrimp paste (bagoong) for serving

Instructions

Cook the meat: Boil oxtail in water until tender (1-2 hours) or use a pressure cooker. Reserve the broth. Sauté base: In a pot, heat oil, add annatto powder, and sauté garlic and onion. Add ingredients: Pour in reserved broth, peanut butter, and ground rice. Stir until thickened. Add veggies: Add banana heart, string beans, and eggplant. Cook until tender. Season: Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Serve: Pair with bagoong and steamed rice.

Adobo

Ingredients

1 kg chicken (or pork, or a mix) 1/2 cup soy sauce 1/4 cup vinegar 1/2 cup water 4 cloves garlic, minced 2-3 dried bay leaves 1 tsp whole peppercorns 1 tbsp oil 1 tbsp sugar (optional)

Instructions

Marinate: Combine chicken, soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns. Marinate for at least 30 minutes. Sauté: Heat oil in a pot, add marinated meat, and cook until lightly browned. Simmer: Add vinegar (do not stir), bay leaves, and water. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 20-30 minutes. Season: Adjust taste with sugar, salt, or more soy sauce, if needed. Serve: Pair with steamed rice.

Sinigang

Ingredients

1 kg pork belly (or ribs, shrimp, or fish) 1 packet sinigang mix (tamarind base) 6 cups water 2 tomatoes, quartered 1 onion, quartered 1 radish, sliced 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) leaves 2 eggplants, sliced 2 okra, sliced (optional) Salt or fish sauce to taste

Instructions

Boil: In a pot, boil pork, onion, and tomatoes in water until the meat is tender. Add veggies: Add radish, eggplant, string beans, and okra. Cook until tender. Flavor: Stir in sinigang mix, adjusting to taste. Add salt or fish sauce if needed. Finish: Add kangkong leaves, simmer for 1-2 minutes, and serve hot with rice.

Halo-Halo

Ingredients

1 cup shaved ice 1/4 cup sweetened jackfruit 1/4 cup sweetened banana (saba) 1/4 cup sweetened purple yam (ube halaya) 1/4 cup sweetened red beans 1/4 cup coconut meat (optional) 1 scoop ube ice cream 1/4 cup leche flan (flan or caramel custard) 1-2 tbsp sweetened condensed milk

Instructions

Layer ingredients: In a tall glass, layer sweetened jackfruit, banana, red beans, ube, and coconut meat (if using). Add ice: Fill the glass with shaved ice, then top with ube ice cream and leche flan. Drizzle: Pour sweetened condensed milk over the ice and ingredients. Mix and serve: Stir everything together and enjoy!

Leche Flan

Ingredients

1 can (395g) sweetened condensed milk 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk 6 large egg yolks 1 tsp vanilla extract 1/2 cup sugar (for caramel)

Instructions

Make caramel: In a pan, melt sugar over medium heat until golden brown. Pour into a round flan mold and tilt to coat evenly. Prepare custard: In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, sweetened condensed milk, evaporated milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Strain mixture: Strain the custard mixture into the caramel-coated mold to remove any egg bits. Steam: Cover the mold with foil and steam over simmering water for 30-40 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool and serve: Let the leche flan cool, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours. Flip onto a plate before serving.

Tortilla Española

Ingredients

4 large potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced 1 medium onion, thinly sliced 6 large eggs 1/4 cup olive oil Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

Cook potatoes and onions: Heat olive oil in a pan, then fry the potatoes and onions over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until tender (about 15 minutes). Drain excess oil. Prepare eggs: In a bowl, beat the eggs, adding salt and pepper. Combine: Add the cooked potatoes and onions to the beaten eggs and mix gently. Cook the tortilla: Heat a little olive oil in a pan. Pour in the egg mixture and cook over low heat for 5-7 minutes, shaking occasionally. Flip: Once the edges are set, place a plate over the pan, flip the tortilla, and slide it back into the pan to cook the other side for 4-5 minutes until fully set. Serve: Let cool slightly, slice, and serve.

Churros

Ingredients

1 cup water 2 tbsp sugar 1/2 tsp salt 2 tbsp vegetable oil 1 cup all-purpose flour 2 large eggs 1/4 cup sugar (for coating) 1 tsp cinnamon (for coating) Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions

Make dough: In a saucepan, combine water, sugar, salt, and oil. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat. Stir in flour until smooth. Let the dough cool slightly, then mix in the eggs one at a time. Heat oil: Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C). Shape churros: Spoon the dough into a piping bag with a star tip. Pipe 4-6 inch strips of dough into the hot oil, cutting them with scissors. Fry until golden and crisp (2-3 minutes). Coat in sugar: Mix the sugar and cinnamon in a bowl. Roll the churros in this mixture right after frying. Serve: Serve warm with chocolate sauce for dipping.

Jamon Ibérico

Ingredient

1 whole leg of Jamón Ibérico (or slices, if available)

Instructions

Serve whole leg: If you have a whole leg of Jamón Ibérico, use a ham stand to stabilize it. Slice thinly with a serrated knife, ensuring each slice is paper-thin for maximum flavor. Serve pre-sliced: If you have pre-sliced Jamón Ibérico, simply arrange the slices on a plate. Pairing: Serve with crusty bread, Manchego cheese, olives, and a glass of Spanish wine (like Rioja or Sherry) for an authentic experience.
